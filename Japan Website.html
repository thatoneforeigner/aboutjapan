Japan

Summary

Having recently visited Japan as a foreigner, I was able to experience so many awesome things and visit various places which I thought was too good not share. I would like to share with you guys that could maybe persuade you to check out Japan (of course with sufficient funds). So here are personally 7 things that I hope will entice you to check out Japan!


  1. Tokyo Skytree Tower




    2. Standing as the TALLEST skycraper in the world is the Tokyo Skytree standing at 634 meters tall.
    Not only is it the tallest tower in the world but it's a great and very popular tourist viewpoint. I believe the view up there to be INCREDIBLE and I suggest that if you want a good city-view of Tokyo then you should definitely check this place out!

  2. Akihabara





    3. If you're a huge fan of modern Japanese culture, specifically anime and its various cafes, then this is just the place for you! Having a wide variety of shops and stores to chose from, Akihabara has most notably places like:
    • Animato
    • Neko Cafe
    • Maid Cafe (The infamous maid cafe lmao)
    • Bic Camera
    • etc...

    And ESPECIALLY If you're a huge anime fan, animato has every corner of every manga covered! you'll enjoy everything about Akihabara! from the eccentric personalities and dresses to the absoluetly adorable cats in the neko cafe and the loads of anime you can find about!

  3. The Ridiculously Tasty Food





    4. If there's any reason you should go to Japan, it HAS to be the food. There's an ENDLESS amount of options to choose from and almost* all of them are perfect imo (except Nato. I have strong feelings about that one).
    With minor exceptions, Japanese food is extremely delicious and healthy as well which means you can devour away and carry on with minimal consequences! Some of my personal favorites are here:

    • Tonkatsu
    • Cup Noodle Ramen
    • Sushi
    • Okonomiyaki
    • Ramen
    • Fried Rice

    If you come to Japan, don't be reluctant to try their food. I PROMISE you won't regret it!

  4. Historical Structures





    5. Japan has a lot of complex and vast historic surrounding its origins. Its historical and religious differences have heavily shaped much of the way Japan operates the way it does. So if you are interested in the historical significance of how Japan came about, definitely check these places out!

  5. Etiquette and Social Culture





    6. Japan has a very sophisticated social strucuture and hierarchy defining the way they function in society. For example, Their everyday speech consists of formal and informal interactions and they follow a different set of rules and morals completely different from America such as Bushido.

    Some of these little cultural differences are:


    • Refraining from things that make you stand out in public settings.

      • In Japan, most people don't yell and abruptly or suddenly make outbursts or any noticeable comments of any kind in an ordeer to maintain cohesiveness as a whole unit instead of unique individuals (Group > Individual).

    • Bow your head. Don't use too much physical touch.

      • Bowing is a customary way of greeting others, apologizing, saying goodbyes, and much more Physical touch often can make Japanese people feel uncomfortable, so please stick to customs by bowing instead.

    • Most importantly...DON'T SIT ON THE FLOOR!

      Okay now this one isn't so much of a rule, I just found it funny because it would keep repeatedly getting mentioned during my trip in Japan. It's true however that not many people in Japan so willingly sit on the floor as they would much rather stand up or try and find a seat instead.


    If you are one who's fascinated with this aspect of Japanese culture, it's definitely worth checking out!

  6. Cleanliness





    7. One very amazing thing about Japan is despite the reliant use on public transportation systems, walking, and cycling mainly to get to locations, Japanese areas are mostly completely clean and devoid of garbage. It's a breath of fresh air if you're from America where everywhere is riddled with bits and pieces of garbage. Here in Japan however, you'll have trouble even finding a piece of garbage. Even weirder, they have LESS garabage bins sitting around than America.
